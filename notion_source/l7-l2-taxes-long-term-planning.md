1. Why Taxes Matter
Taxes fund schools, hospitals, and roads. They affect how much money you actually keep from your income. When you earn money, you do not keep every dollar — a portion goes to the government for public services. Understanding taxes helps you plan better, avoid surprises, and make smarter decisions.

2. Types of Taxes
- Income Tax: paid based on how much you earn from a job or business. The more you earn, the higher the percentage you might owe. E.g. earning $30,000/year → roughly 10–15% owed depending on where you live.
- Payroll Tax: automatic paycheck deductions funding Social Security and Medicare. Taken out before you get your salary.
- Sales Tax: added to prices of things you buy. Small per item, adds up.
- Property Tax: yearly tax on a house or land you own, based on value.
- Capital Gains Tax: tax on profit when you sell an investment for more than you paid.

3. How Taxes Affect Your Income
Earn $2,000/month gross → after income + payroll taxes you might receive about $1,700.
Gross income = total earned. Net income = what you can actually use.
Budget on net, not gross — prevents overspending and under-saving.

4. Planning for the Future Beyond the Paycheck
- Emergency Fund: 3–6 months of expenses.
- Retirement: start early; compound interest = interest on interest + on prior interest.
  Example: $100/month from age 20 at 7% → nearly $240,000 by 65; from age 30 → about $120,000. [NUMBERS WRONG — see header]
- Long-Term Goals: write down big goals (house, business, travel); estimate costs and timelines; build your budget around them.

5. Managing Your Money Year After Year
Review yearly: Did income change? Saving enough for goals? Adjust budget for new priorities?
Consistent review prevents debt, reduces stress, increases freedom.

6. Smart Habits for Long-Term Success
- Track expenses monthly.
- Avoid unnecessary debt; pay credit cards in full.
- Keep learning about taxes, investments, budgeting.
- Celebrate progress — small wins matter.

7. Real-Life Reflection
- How do taxes affect how I plan my budget?
- What can I do today to prepare for big future goals?
- How do I balance saving for tomorrow with enjoying today?
