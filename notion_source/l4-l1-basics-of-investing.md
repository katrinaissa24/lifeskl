What is Investing?
Investing is when you use your money today to try to make more money in the future. Unlike spending, where your money disappears after buying something, investing aims to grow your wealth over time.
- You spend $10 on a video game → it's gone forever.
- You invest $10 in a stock → if it grows, that $10 could become $15, $20, or more.
Investing is like planting a tree. You plant a seed, water it, and take care of it. Over time, it grows into a tree that gives fruit. The fruit is the extra money you earn.

Why Invest Instead of Just Saving?
Saving keeps your money safe, but it grows very slowly.
- $100 in a regular bank account with 1% interest → $101 after a year.
- $100 invested with 8% average growth → could be $108 after a year.
Investing comes with risk (you could lose money), but also reward.

Different Types of Investments

Stocks
- You own a small piece of a company.
- If the company grows, your stock increases in value. Some pay dividends (a portion of profit).
- Example: 1 share of Nike = a tiny part of Nike.
- Real-life comparison: Owning a piece of your favorite pizza shop. If it becomes popular, your slice's value goes up.
- Risk: Volatile. Prices go up and down daily.

Bonds
- Loans you give to a company or government. They pay you back later, with interest.
- Example: $1,000 government bond at 3% → in 5 years, you get $1,150. [simple interest framing]
- Real-life comparison: Lending your friend $100 and they return $110.
- Risk: Usually safer than stocks, but not risk-free (bankruptcy).

Mutual Funds
- Collections of many stocks and bonds managed by professionals.
- Your money is spread across all investments in the fund.
- Real-life comparison: Not putting all your eggs in one basket.
- Risk: Lower than individual stocks, growth usually slower.

Risk vs. Reward
- High risk = chance to make more (stocks, crypto).
- Low risk = safer, slower (government bonds).
- Tip: Diversify to reduce risk.

The Power of Time & Compound Interest
- Start at 18: invest $50/month → by 65, could be $250,000+ (depending on growth).
- Start at 30: same $50/month → by 65, only around $100,000.
It's like rolling a snowball down a hill: the longer it rolls, the bigger it gets.

How to Start Investing Even with Small Money
- Micro-investing apps ($5–$10 at a time).
- Low-cost index funds or ETFs (track the whole market).
- Avoid "get rich quick" schemes.
- Keep emergency cash in savings BEFORE investing.

Key Takeaways
- Investing = planting money today to grow in the future.
- Stocks = ownership, higher reward/risk. Bonds = lending, safer. Mutual funds = mix.
- Start early → time + compound interest = massive advantage.
- Diversify. Be patient — wealth grows slowly.

## Game time — Investing Adventure (portfolio simulation)

Starting Money: $1,000. Decide allocation among:
- Stocks (High Risk/Reward): monthly change –20% to +30%.
- Bonds (Low): +1% to +3%.
- Mutual Funds (Medium): –5% to +10%.
✅ Best: Diversify — e.g., $500 stocks, $300 bonds, $200 mutual funds.

Market events:
- Tech Boom → stocks +20%. Sell to lock profit or hold? Best: hold moderately, sell small portion.
- Market Crash → stocks –15%. Panic sell or stay? Best: stay invested; stocks often recover.
- Interest Rate Hike → bonds +2%, stocks –5%. Best: keep some stocks, maybe shift slightly to bonds.

Unexpected expenses:
- Emergency car repair $200 → withdraw from bonds/mutual funds (safer, smaller long-term impact).
- Cool gadget $150 → let investments grow if emergency fund exists.

End of year scoring: $1,200+ Excellent; $1,000–$1,199 Good; below $1,000 Needs Improvement (panic sold, overspent, or didn't diversify).
